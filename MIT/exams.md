# Exam operations at MIT

Cross-MIT exam-ops notes: proctoring philosophy, conflicts, accommodations. EECS-specific room / scanning flow lives in [EECS/exam-day.md](EECS/exam-day.md).

## Proctoring philosophy

Default behavior, adapted from Leslie Kaelbling's guidance:

- Mostly don't give out information when answering questions; discourage them.
- Be alert to errors or serious ambiguities in the exam.
- Obvious confirmation questions are okay to answer ("By X do you mean Y?"). For anything more complicated, tell the student to write down their interpretation and move on.
- If a question recurs (2-3 students ask), make a clarifying announcement to the room. Coordinate across rooms so all proctors stay in sync.
- If an error is too bad to fix in a few words, announce that students should skip the problem.
- Late students: that's on them. Sick / distraught students: encourage them to take care of themselves and follow up via course staff afterwards.
- Sit at the front and have question-askers come to you. Disrupts fewer students; tends to generate fewer questions.
- Make everyone finish on time.

## During the exam

- Display the [extended-time countdown clock](https://shenshen.mit.edu/clock/) on the room projector. (See [Tools](README.md#tools).)
- Write the time remaining on the board every 30 minutes. Verbal announcement at 15 min and 5 min remaining.
- Restroom breaks are fine, but students leave phones and devices behind.
- Count students in the room; reconcile with collected exam count after.

## Exam conflicts

The Registrar schedules a [Conflict Exam](https://registrar.mit.edu/classes-grades-evaluations/examinations/final-exam-schedule/conflict-exams) for finals. Cross-registered students with schedule conflicts can use that slot. For midterms, route requests through your course-personal email alias.

## DAS accommodations

- Approved accommodations come from [Disability and Access Services (DAS)](https://studentlife.mit.edu/das). Have students forward their DAS approvals to your course-personal email alias.
- For exam-day accommodations (extended time, separate room, etc.), require notice at least one week before the exam.
- Mid-term extensions tied to disability are typically handled separately from the regular extension policy; coordinate with DAS.

## Illness and personal issues

- Sick students: do not attend class or in-person OH; route through your course-personal email alias for accommodation.
- Personal issues affecting attendance / progress: students should see a Dean in [Student Support Services (S³)](https://studentlife.mit.edu/s3) first, then loop in the course via the course-personal alias.
- Course staff should *not* handle these directly via personal email; funnel them.
