# Recording lectures at MIT

How lectures get captured and where they land. After recording, [../LMS/panopto.md](../LMS/panopto.md) covers pulling videos out via the Panopto API.

Three options, roughly in order of human touch needed:

## 1. Default: contact MIT Open Learning

For a standard in-person lecture, the entry point is the **MIT Open Learning team**. They'll route you based on the room and your needs (camera setup, captioning, where the recording lands).

## 3. Self-service: Lightweight Lecture Capture (LWLC)

IS&T offers automated, unattended capture in ~50 Registrar classrooms (~39 also livestream). No cost. Sign up via the **MIT Atlas app**: the "Lecture Capture" tile lists any subject you're scheduled to teach if at least one section is in an equipped room. You can schedule recurring recordings, add co-instructors, and cancel sessions in-app.

- **Equipped rooms list**: [ist.mit.edu/lecture-capture](https://ist.mit.edu/lecture-capture).
- **Setup guide**: [Lightweight Lecture Capture Guide (kb.mit.edu)](http://kb.mit.edu/confluence/display/istcontrib/Lightweight+Lecture+Capture+Guide).
- **Support**: `lwlc-support@mit.edu`, 617-324-3578.
- **Output**: per-session links emailed for webcast, recording, and downloads.

If your room isn't on the LWLC list and you'd like it added, contact `lwlc-support@mit.edu`. To request a different room assignment, contact the Registrar at `schedules@mit.edu`. Technician-assisted capture is also available through MIT Video Productions (MVP) at a per-class fee; see `mvp.mit.edu`.
